My Screenprinting project

This Screenprint Project was a project that we used in order to voice our solutions and thoughts about problems occuring all over the world. Many people talked about equity, funding, personal life and more, but my project focuses on the world as a whole, my project is based on the message; "look closely". As this story proceeds, that message will begin to make sense.

PhotoShop

This project began in Photoshop. Our first task with this project was coming up with a screenprint design that we wanted to use to visualize the message we wanted to get across. My idea was to use an eye with an image of the world inside, because you use your eyes to look just like my message, but when you "look closely" into the eye, you can see the Earth. Diving deeper into this means that the problems around the world are right infront of our faces, but we just have to open our eyes and notice it.

Adobe Illustrator

After finishing our photoshop design, we moved onto a program called Adobe Illustrator. In Illustrator, we made our wooden frame that we wanted to place our soon to be screenprinted design into. In this program, we had to determine the lengths of each wooden frame, as well as measure the dimensions of our vinyl and print because if any of our numbers where off, in the end our paint would leak through and ruin everything. Some can say Illustrator was the most Important stage in our whole process.

Frame Building

Using our Illustrator measurements, frame building was up next. Mr.Sinusas provided us with long strips of wood that we were able to cut to our dimensions using a miter saw. This stage of woodwork required precise cuts and marking. When all said and done, actually putting the wood together was next. Glue and staples were used to piece the corners together to give us a complete rectangular frame. Afterwards, we streched,taped,and stapled a piece of mesh to our frame in order for our vinyl screenprint to be displayed.

Silhouette Studios

In silhouette studios, we exported an image of our design and resized it to fit in our frame with a .5in gap from the design to the wood. When our design was sized properly, we grabbed a piece of vinyl and inserted into the vinyl cutting machine. We then linked our computers to the machine and the machine began transcribing our design onto the vinyl. This proccess took about 5-10 minutes.

Final assembly

In the final stages of building our framework, we placed our vinyl ontop of the mesh and began heat pressing it into the mesh using an ironer. Applying continous pressure was necessary so that our vinyl design wouldn't fall of the mesh, but instead it felt like it was apart of the mesh. When we finished with this step, we began taping the edges and corners of our frame in order to prevent any paint from leaking through our mesh.

Print Transferring

The paint explained previously was used in order to transfer our design from our frame onto a piece of paper,fabric, and even a box. The box was used for our personal use and our review cards. The fabric was used to make a shared canvas with everyone elses designs in the class, and the piece of paper was just to give us an example of what the transferring process was like. Transferring our design onto these 3 objects required;

  • squeegee
  • paint
  • 3 objects of transfer
